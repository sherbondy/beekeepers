Our Mission

We intend to foster community awareness and understanding of the honeybee (apis mellifera), a remarkable living model of social organization. We adhere to mens et manus: the MIT Beekeeping Club strives for practical, hands-on interaction with honeybees through the upkeep of hives, coupled with intellectual enrichment by viewing bees as a lens for doing science.

Ongoing Projects

pika Colony

This is the first of three planned hives in the MIT community. It lives in the backyard of pika (the MIT ILG).

If you have ideas for where to host the next two hives, get in touch! We will be in ongoing discussions this semester with MIT Facilities to find a suitable home.

Experimental Hive Fabrication

We intend to use the CNC routing equipment at the MIT Hobby Shop or one of the Fab Labs on campus to construct some Open Source Beehives (the Warré design should perform best in the northeast winters). We may experiment with customizing the designs to suit our purposes!
